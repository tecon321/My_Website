Human Gene Disease Network Analysis

Overview:

During my final year of college I took a class called Biological networks. For my final project, I built a graphical representation of the OMIM human gene disease network, analyzed its network structure and predicted missing links between genes and genetic disorders.


Read Full Research Paper

Project Outline

Phase Description Technologies Used Images
Data Collection The data I used for this was the 'Human Disease Network': a network of all known genetic disorders and associated genes developed by OMIM. I imported the network into Cytoscape, converted it into GraphML file, and loaded into Python as a bipartite NetworkX graph. Python, NetworkX, Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Cytoscape
Constructing Graphs After importing the netowork into Python, I constructed two single mode representations of the graph; the HDN and GDN. For the HDN, nodes were all genetic disorders, and two disorders are linked if they share a gene whose mutation is implicated in both disorders. In the GDN, Genes are the nodes and two genes are linked if their mutations are implicated in a shared disorder. Python, NetworkX, NumPy
Link Prediction Evaluation Once all of the graphs were constructed, I wanted to analyze the graph structure and determine how accurately missing links in the network could be predicted. To do this I reconstructed each graph while excluding various proportions of known links. I then ran three link prediction algorithms to determine the most likely missing links. The three algorithms included a Jaccard predictor, degree product predictor, and a random baseline predictor. After several test I found that the Jaccard predictor was able to find missing links the most accurately, yielding an AUC score of 0.947. Python, NetworkX, NumPy
Results After proving that missing links could be predicted well using my Jaccard predictor, I was able to conclude that there was high clustering in the orignial network, and that 'bridge' nodes were most likely to have missing links in the network. A bridge node is classified as a disorder or gene that has links to nodes in multiple disorder classes. Python, NetworkX, NumPy
